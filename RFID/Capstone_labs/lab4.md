# Lab 4: Card Handling, Procurement, and Decommissioning
**Authorized Use Only**

## Objective
Teach proper administrative handling of lab cards and explain how to avoid mixing training media with production access credentials.

## Learning Outcomes
By the end of this lab, students should be able to:
- Properly label and track lab cards
- Store test credentials safely
- Separate lab assets from production assets
- Document retirement or destruction of lab media

## Prerequisites
- A set of lab-owned RFID cards or fobs
- Basic inventory tracking method
- Approval to manage training inventory

## Procedure

### Step 1: Label all lab cards
Use a consistent naming format, such as:
- `LAB-LF-001`
- `LAB-HF-001`
- `LAB-DUAL-001`

### Step 2: Maintain an inventory log
Track:
- Card label
- Frequency
- Technology family
- Assigned storage location
- Current status

### Step 3: Store test media separately
Keep lab cards in a clearly marked container and do not mix them with:
- Employee badges
- Tenant badges
- Vendor-issued production credentials
- Temporary visitor access cards

### Step 4: Use approved procurement channels
When more cards are needed:
- Purchase clearly designated **lab-only** cards
- Log them when received
- Store them with the rest of training inventory

### Step 5: Decommission safely
When a lab card is retired:
- Mark it as retired in the inventory
- Remove it from circulation
- Destroy or store it according to internal policy

> **Note:** Production credentials should only be revoked, reissued, or decommissioned through the authorized physical security or vendor workflow.

## Check for Understanding
1. Why should lab cards and production cards never be mixed?
2. What information should always be tracked in an inventory log?
3. Why is decommissioning documentation important?
4. What team should manage live production credential changes?
